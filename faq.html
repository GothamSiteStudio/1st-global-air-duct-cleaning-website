Air Duct Cleaning FAQs

Everything You've Wanted to Ask

How often should air ducts be cleaned?

The National Air Duct Cleaners Association (NADCA) recommends professional cleaning every 3 to 7 years for most homes. You may need it more often if you have pets that shed, family members with allergies or asthma, recent home renovations, or you've moved into a previously occupied home. In St. Louis, our humid summers and pollen-heavy springs can shorten that interval for many homes.

How much does air duct cleaning cost in St. Louis?

Cost depends on the size of the home, the number of vents, the condition of the duct system, and whether you have one or two HVAC units. We give a free, no-obligation written estimate before any work begins so you'll know exactly what you'll pay. Call (314) 718-3632 for a quote tailored to your home.

How long does air duct cleaning take?

A typical residential cleaning takes 2 to 4 hours, depending on home size, number of vents, condition of the ductwork, and whether you have one or two HVAC systems. Larger homes (3,000+ sq ft) or homes with heavy buildup may take longer. We always give a realistic time estimate when we quote the job.

Is air duct cleaning worth it?

For most homes, yes. Professional cleaning removes dust, pet dander, mold spores, allergens, and debris that accumulate in the HVAC system. The benefits: cleaner indoor air, fewer allergy and asthma symptoms, better HVAC efficiency, and longer equipment life. Homes with pets, smokers, recent renovations, or people with respiratory conditions see the biggest benefit.

What is included in a professional cleaning?

Our standard air duct cleaning includes:

  • Pre-cleaning camera inspection
  • Cleaning of all supply and return ducts
  • Hand-cleaning of registers, grilles, and diffusers
  • Vacuuming of the air handler blower compartment (where accessible)
  • HEPA-filtered industrial vacuum extraction
  • Rotary brush agitation for stubborn buildup
  • Final walkthrough with before/after photos

Do you clean dryer vents too?

Yes. Dryer vent cleaning is one of our two core services. We clean the full vent line from the dryer to the outside termination, inspect the outside vent cap, check connections at the joints, and run an airflow test afterward. Annual dryer vent cleaning is one of the most important fire-prevention tasks for any home the U.S. Fire Administration reports nearly 2,900 dryer fires every year.

How do I know if my dryer vent needs cleaning?

Watch for these warning signs:

  • Clothes take more than one cycle to fully dry
  • The dryer feels excessively hot to the touch
  • You smell something burning when the dryer runs
  • You see lint piling up around the dryer or outside vent
  • It's been more than a year since the last cleaning

If any of these are happening, schedule a cleaning right away lint buildup is the leading cause of dryer fires.

Will air duct cleaning help with allergies and asthma?

It can help significantly. Ducts collect dust mites, pet dander, pollen, mold spores, and other allergens. When your HVAC runs, those particles cycle through every room. Removing them at the source often reduces sneezing, congestion, and asthma triggers especially during spring and fall when pollen counts in St. Louis are high.

Can air duct cleaning improve HVAC efficiency?

Yes. When ducts and the air handler blower are clogged, your HVAC works harder to push air through the home. The result: higher utility bills, more wear on the equipment, and uneven heating or cooling. Removing that buildup helps the system breathe most homeowners notice better airflow and more even temperatures after a thorough cleaning.

Will the cleaning process make a mess in my house?

No. We use HEPA-filtered industrial vacuums that capture contaminants inside the equipment instead of releasing them back into your home. We protect the area around vent openings, and we leave each work area as clean as or cleaner than we found it. A walkthrough at the end confirms you're happy before we leave.
