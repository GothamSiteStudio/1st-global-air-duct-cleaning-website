Dryer Vent Cleaning in St. Louis, MO

The single most important fire-prevention task for any home with a dryer done quickly, safely, and professionally by a family-owned team trusted since 2014.

Why It Matters

Dryer Vent Cleaning Saves Lives

According to the U.S. Fire Administration, dryer fires cause nearly 2,900 home fires every year resulting in injuries, deaths, and over $35 million in property damage. The leading cause? Lint buildup in the dryer vent.

The lint trap inside your dryer catches only a fraction of the lint your clothes produce. The rest builds up inside the vent line slowly, year after year until airflow is restricted, the dryer overheats, and that lint becomes fuel. Annual cleaning is the most effective and affordable fire-prevention task for any home.

Warning Signs

Signs Your Dryer Vent Needs Cleaning Right Now

If any of these are happening in your home, schedule a cleaning right away these are the warning signs of a vent that's becoming dangerous.

  • Clothes take longer than one cycle to dry The most common sign of a clogged vent.
  • The dryer feels excessively hot to the touch Restricted airflow makes the unit overheat.
  • Burning smell when the dryer runs Lint scorching against the heating element. Stop using the dryer and call us immediately.
  • Lint visible around the dryer or outside vent Backed-up lint is escaping where it shouldn't.
  • It's been more than a year since the last cleaning Whatever you can see, there's more inside the line.
  • The lint trap fills up faster than usual A telltale sign of restricted airflow throughout the system.
  • The laundry room feels hotter or more humid than normal during a cycle Heat that should be venting outside is staying inside.

What's Included in Our Dryer Vent Cleaning

Every job, no shortcuts. Here's exactly what we do.

  • Disconnect and inspect the dryer We pull the dryer out, disconnect the vent, and inspect the connection at the back of the unit.
  • Full vent line cleaning From the dryer connection all the way to the outside termination.

  • Airflow test We verify proper airflow before we leave so you know the system is performing.
  • Reconnection and final test run Dryer reconnected, secured, and tested. We don't leave until it's running like new.

Why It Matters

Benefits of Annual Dryer Vent Cleaning

Fire Prevention

Lint is highly flammable. Removing it from the vent line eliminates the leading cause of dryer fires.

Faster Drying

Clothes dry in one cycle instead of two. Most homeowners notice the difference the first load they run.

Lower Energy Bills

A dryer that needs to run twice uses twice the electricity or gas. Restoring airflow cuts that waste.

Longer Dryer Life

Less heat stress on the heating element and motor. A clean vent extends the working life of an expensive appliance.

Less Humidity

A clogged vent leaks moisture into the laundry room. A clean line vents that humidity outside where it belongs.

Peace of Mind

You'll know the single most overlooked fire risk in your home is handled for less than the cost of an insurance deductible.

Quick Answers

Common Questions About Dryer Vent Cleaning

How often should dryer vents be cleaned?

At least once per year for most households. Families that do laundry every day or have long vent runs (common in newer construction) should consider every 6 months.

How long does the cleaning take?

Typically 30 to 60 minutes. Longer vent runs or vents with significant blockages may take a bit more time. We'll give you a realistic estimate over the phone.

Do you offer same-day service?

Yes same-day appointments are often available, especially when you call before 2:00 PM. Call (314) 718-3632 and we'll let you know our earliest opening.

Will you move my dryer?

Yes. We'll pull the dryer out from the wall to access the vent connection, clean everything, then reconnect and push it back. We protect floors so we don't scratch hardwood or tile.

What if my vent runs through a wall or ceiling?

That's standard for most homes. Our equipment handles long, multi-bend vent lines that go through walls, ceilings, or up through the roof. We clean the entire line from the dryer to the outside termination.
