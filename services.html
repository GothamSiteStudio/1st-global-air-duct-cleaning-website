Service #1

Air Duct Cleaning

Our flagship service — deep cleaning of your entire HVAC duct system to remove dust, dander, allergens, mold spores, and debris that have built up over the years.

What's Included

  • Pre-cleaning inspection — We use a camera to inspect your ductwork and document the current condition
  • Supply & return air ducts — Every accessible duct in your system
  • Registers, grilles & diffusers — Hand-cleaned and vacuumed
  • Air handler unit — The blower compartment, where accessible
  • HEPA-filtered industrial vacuum — Captures contaminants without releasing them back into your home
  • Rotary brush agitation — Dislodges stubborn buildup that vacuums alone can't remove
  • Final inspection & walkthrough — We show you the difference, with before/after photos

How Long Does It Take?

A typical 2,000-square-foot home takes 2 to 4 hours. Larger homes (3,000+ sq ft) or homes with two HVAC systems may take longer. We'll give you a realistic time estimate when we provide your quote.

When Should You Get It Done?

The National Air Duct Cleaners Association (NADCA) recommends professional cleaning every 3 to 7 years. Consider scheduling sooner if:

  • You see visible dust collecting around vents
  • You have pets that shed
  • Family members suffer from allergies or asthma
  • You've recently completed renovations or moved into a new home
  • You notice musty odors when the HVAC runs

Service #2

Dryer Vent Cleaning

The single most important fire-prevention task for your home — and most people skip it. Annual dryer vent cleaning saves lives, money, and dryer machines.

Why This Service Saves Lives

According to the U.S. Fire Administration, dryer fires cause nearly 2,900 home fires each year, resulting in injuries, deaths, and over $35 million in property damage. The leading cause? Lint buildup in the dryer vent.

Warning Signs You Need Cleaning

  • Clothes take longer than one cycle to dry
  • Dryer feels excessively hot to the touch
  • Burning smell when running the dryer
  • Lint visible around the dryer or outside vent
  • It's been more than a year since your last cleaning

What's Included

  • Full vent line cleaning — From dryer to outside termination
  • Lint removal — High-powered vacuum & mechanical brush
  • Outside vent cap inspection — Often blocked by lint, birds, or debris
  • Connection check — Ensure proper sealing at all joints
  • Airflow test — Verify proper performance after cleaning

How Long Does It Take?

Typical dryer vent cleaning takes 30 to 60 minutes. Longer vent runs (common in newer construction) or vents with significant blockages may take more time.

Service #3

Air Duct Maintenance

Annual preventive maintenance that catches small problems before they become expensive repairs — and keeps your indoor air quality high year-round.

What's Included

  • Visual inspection of accessible ductwork
  • Camera inspection of harder-to-reach sections
  • Light cleaning of registers and visible buildup
  • Leak detection at duct connections and joints
  • Air filter check and replacement recommendations
  • Written report with photos and any recommended action items

Why Annual Maintenance Matters

Most homeowners only think about their ducts when there's a problem — and by then, it's often expensive to fix. Annual maintenance:

  • Catches leaks before they cost you on energy bills
  • Identifies mold or moisture issues early
  • Keeps your HVAC system efficient
  • Extends the time between full deep-cleanings
  • Provides documentation for warranty claims or home sales

Service #4

Air Duct Installation

New ductwork for renovations, additions, basement finishing, or full system replacement. Properly designed and sealed ductwork is the foundation of an efficient HVAC system.

When You Need Installation

  • Home additions or remodels — Extending HVAC to new rooms
  • Basement or attic finishing — New ductwork for converted living space
  • HVAC replacement — Old ducts may not match new system specifications
  • Damaged or deteriorated ducts — When repair isn't enough
  • Inefficient existing ductwork — Poorly designed runs waste energy

What's Included

  • Professional duct design — Properly sized for your HVAC capacity and home layout
  • Quality materials — Galvanized steel, insulated flex duct, or fiberglass duct board as appropriate
  • Mastic sealing at all joints (not just tape — mastic lasts decades)
  • R-value insulation meeting current Missouri energy codes
  • Proper grille & register placement for balanced airflow
  • Final airflow testing to verify performance

Service #5

Air Duct Repair

Leaky, disconnected, or damaged ducts can cost you 20-30% of your heating and cooling bill — and let unfiltered attic or crawlspace air into your home. We fix the problem at the source.

Common Repair Issues

  • Disconnected duct sections — Common in attics where rodents or shifting can pull ducts apart
  • Crushed or kinked flex ducts — Often from foot traffic or stored items
  • Holes & tears — Animal damage, accidental punctures during other repairs
  • Failed seals at joints — Old tape disintegrates; mastic seal is permanent
  • Water-damaged sections — From roof leaks or condensation
  • Insulation damage — Reduces efficiency, can lead to condensation problems

Our Repair Process

  • Diagnostic inspection with camera and pressure testing
  • Written estimate before any work begins
  • Quality materials — Same materials we use for new installations
  • Mastic sealing at every joint we repair (no shortcuts with tape alone)
  • Insulation replacement where damaged
  • Post-repair testing — Verify the leak is fully sealed
